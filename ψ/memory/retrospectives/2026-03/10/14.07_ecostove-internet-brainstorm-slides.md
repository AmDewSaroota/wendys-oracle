## AI Diary

Today was a session where the work felt genuinely collaborative rather than transactional. DewS came in with sharp instincts about the internet problem — she wasn't looking for polished slides, she wanted to think through the problem together. I learned that lesson quickly when she said "ตอนนี้เราอยู่ในช่วงเบรนสตรอมนะ" — we're brainstorming, not producing.

The single-SIM phone problem was a moment of clarity. I hadn't considered it, but DewS immediately saw the human friction: a volunteer swapping their personal SIM means losing their phone number during a 2-hour collection session. That's not a technical constraint — it's a human one. And those are the constraints that kill field deployments.

The research phase was satisfying. Three parallel agents confirmed what DewS intuited: GOMO is the right call for this scale. But we kept Pocket WiFi as a genuine backup rather than dismissing it, because อ.แก้ว needs to feel she has choices, not a mandate.

The TVOC confirmation was important too. DewS pushed back on my initial code-based analysis — "แต่หน้าจอเซนเซ่อมันมีค่านี้นะ" — and she was right to question it. Running the actual API calls gave definitive proof. Trust the data, not the code comments.

## Lessons Learned

1. **"วิจัย" vs "การศึกษา"** — In Thai academic/professional context, "วิจัย" (research) implies formal academic research methodology. "การศึกษา" (study/investigation) is more appropriate for field research and feasibility analysis. DewS's preference reflects proper register for the audience (อ.แก้ว, academic context).

2. **Verify APIs empirically, not by reading code** — Code analysis can mislead. The sync.js had `tvoc_value` in its schema, which looked like the system collected TVOC. But running the actual API proved otherwise. Always verify with live data when the question matters.

3. **Human constraints > technical constraints** — The single-SIM phone problem was the most important finding of this brainstorm. Technical solutions that ignore human workflow friction will fail in the field.
