# Lesson: Building Presentations for Non-Native English Speakers

**Date**: 2026-04-16
**Source**: 3D Pipeline presentation session with DewS
**Tags**: presentation, english, communication, non-native

## Pattern

When creating presentation content for someone who will present in their second language:

### 1. Word-Level Review is Essential
- Check every key phrase for pronunciation difficulty
- "Hinge", "crack open", "vague" — all common English words that tripped up an intermediate speaker
- Offer alternatives using simpler, more universal vocabulary
- Test: can the presenter say it naturally in conversation?

### 2. Ask Their Words First, Then Polish
- Don't generate 4+ polished options from YOUR assumptions
- Ask "how would YOU say it?" — even in their native language
- Then refine their actual phrasing into smoother English
- Result is faster, more authentic, and easier for them to deliver

### 3. Content Must Feel Like Theirs
- "Learning to model is the easy part" — DewS rejected this because it felt dismissive of her craft
- The presenter must OWN the message, not just read it
- Professional values > clever copywriting

### 4. Warm Up in the Target Language
- DewS switched our conversation to English deliberately before the presentation
- This is a smart technique: test words in casual conversation before putting them on slides
- The assistant should match this energy and respond naturally in that language

## Anti-Pattern
- Generating multiple fancy options with idioms and complex phrasing
- Assuming intermediate speakers know words like "hinge", "beholder", "crack open"
- Prioritizing how text READS over how it SOUNDS when spoken aloud
