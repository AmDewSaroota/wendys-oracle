## What You Need to Prepare

### 1. Pick ONE Project
Choose a project you know well and can talk about comfortably. It doesn't have to be your best — it has to be one with a good STORY.

### 2. Prepare These Visuals
For your chosen project, we need:

| Visual | What it shows |
|--------|--------------|
| **Reference photo/concept** | What you started with |
| **Blockout screenshot** | The ugly beginning — boxes and cylinders |
| **Wireframe view** | Clean topology (or messy — be honest!) |
| **Final shaded model** | The finished mesh before handoff |

Take screenshots from Maya/Blender with a **dark gray background**, same angle for wireframe and shaded if possible.

### 3. Think About These Questions
- What was the hardest part of this project?
- Did you make any mistakes? How did you fix them?
- Did you need to communicate something to the texture artist or animator? What?
- If you could redo this project, what would you do differently?

### 4. Prepare for Q&A
Students might ask you:
- "What software should I learn first?"
- "How long did it take you to get to this level?"
- "Do you use AI tools?"
- "What's the hardest thing about working in a team?"

Be honest. "I don't know" is a perfectly good answer.
